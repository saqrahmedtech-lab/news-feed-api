World leaders gathered in Geneva have signed a landmark accord committing 195 nations to net-zero carbon emissions by 2040, five years ahead of previous targets. The deal includes a $2 trillion green transition fund for developing economies and binding annual review mechanisms that critics and advocates alike call unprecedented.
